Legal Resources & Bar Associations

National Organizations

  • American Bar Association (ABA) - National lawyer organization with referral services
  • American Association for Justice (AAJ) - Leading trial lawyers organization
  • American Board of Professional Liability Attorneys (ABPLA) - Certifies medical malpractice specialists

State Bar Associations

Every state has a bar association that maintains lawyer referral services and can help you find qualified medical malpractice attorneys in your area. Search for "[Your State] Bar Association" to find your state's organization.

Legal Aid Organizations

While medical malpractice cases are typically handled on contingency (no upfront costs), if you need general legal advice or assistance, contact your local legal aid organization.

Patient Safety Organizations

The Joint Commission

The Joint Commission accredits and certifies healthcare organizations. You can file complaints about patient safety issues and verify a facility's accreditation status.

Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS)

CMS oversees quality and safety in healthcare facilities that receive Medicare/Medicaid funding. You can file complaints about care quality.

State Medical Boards

Every state has a medical board that licenses and disciplines physicians. You can file complaints about physician conduct and verify a doctor's license status.

Medical Records

How to Obtain Your Medical Records

Under HIPAA, you have a right to your medical records. To obtain them:

  • Submit a written request to the medical records department of each healthcare provider
  • Include your full name, date of birth, dates of service, and specific records requested
  • Include a signed authorization
  • Provide a valid photo ID
  • Pay any reasonable copying fees
Timeline: Providers typically have 30 days to respond to medical records requests. If they fail to provide records, you can file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights.

What to Request

Request complete medical records including:

  • Doctor's notes and progress reports
  • Nursing notes
  • Laboratory test results
  • Imaging studies (X-rays, MRIs, CT scans)
  • Pathology reports
  • Medication records
  • Surgical reports and operative notes
  • Anesthesia records
  • Emergency room records
  • Hospital discharge summaries

Support Resources

Emotional Support

Dealing with medical injuries and pursuing legal claims can be emotionally taxing. Consider:

  • Speaking with a mental health professional or counselor
  • Joining support groups for people with similar injuries or conditions
  • Connecting with patient advocacy organizations
  • Seeking support from family and friends

Financial Assistance

If you're struggling financially due to medical bills:

  • Ask hospitals about financial assistance programs or payment plans
  • Contact patient advocacy organizations that may provide grants
  • Explore disability benefits if your injury prevents you from working
  • Investigate charitable organizations related to your specific condition

Important Documents to Gather

If you're considering a medical malpractice claim, start gathering:

Medical Documentation

  • Complete medical records from all providers involved in your care
  • All imaging studies (X-rays, MRIs, CT scans) on CD if possible
  • Laboratory and test results
  • Prescription records
  • Hospital billing statements

Financial Documentation

  • All medical bills and invoices
  • Receipts for out-of-pocket medical expenses
  • Pay stubs showing lost wages
  • Documentation of travel expenses for medical care
  • Bills for medical equipment or home modifications

Personal Documentation

  • Journal documenting your symptoms, pain levels, and limitations
  • Photos of visible injuries or conditions
  • Contact information for any witnesses
  • Timeline of events related to your treatment
  • Any correspondence with healthcare providers

Understanding Healthcare Quality Ratings

Hospital Compare

Medicare's Hospital Compare tool (medicare.gov/hospitalcompare) allows you to compare hospitals based on quality measures, patient satisfaction, and safety records.

Physician Compare

Medicare's Physician Compare tool helps you find and compare doctors based on specialty, location, and quality measures.

Leapfrog Group

The Leapfrog Group provides hospital safety grades and quality ratings based on error prevention and patient safety practices.

Questions About Your Rights?

Patient Rights Resources:
  • Your state's Department of Health maintains information about patient rights
  • Hospital patient advocates can help explain your rights and resolve concerns
  • Patient advocacy organizations provide guidance on navigating healthcare
  • Legal aid organizations can provide general information about your rights

State-Specific Resources

Every state has unique laws and resources. To find state-specific information:

  • Contact your state bar association's lawyer referral service
  • Visit your state's medical board website to verify physician licenses
  • Check your state's Department of Health for complaint procedures
  • Review your state's Attorney General website for consumer protection resources
