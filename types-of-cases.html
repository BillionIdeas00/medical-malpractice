Types of Medical Malpractice Cases

Medical malpractice can take many forms, from diagnostic errors to surgical mistakes to medication errors. Understanding common types of malpractice cases can help you recognize if you have been a victim of medical negligence.

1. Misdiagnosis and Delayed Diagnosis

Diagnostic errors are among the most common types of medical malpractice. These occur when a healthcare provider:

  • Fails to diagnose a condition that should have been detected
  • Misdiagnoses a condition as something else
  • Delays diagnosis, allowing the condition to worsen
  • Fails to order appropriate diagnostic tests
  • Misinterprets test results

Commonly Misdiagnosed Conditions

  • Cancer (various types)
  • Heart attacks and cardiovascular disease
  • Stroke
  • Infections (including sepsis)
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Aortic dissection
  • Meningitis
  • Appendicitis

A misdiagnosis can lead to:

  • Delayed or improper treatment
  • Disease progression that could have been prevented
  • Unnecessary procedures or treatments
  • Permanent injury or death

2. Surgical Errors

Surgical malpractice encompasses mistakes made before, during, or after surgery. These errors can have devastating consequences.

Common Surgical Errors Include:

  • Wrong-site surgery: Operating on the wrong body part, side, or patient
  • Foreign objects left inside: Surgical instruments, sponges, or other items left in the body
  • Unnecessary surgery: Performing procedures that aren't medically necessary
  • Nerve damage: Inadvertent injury to nerves during surgery
  • Organ puncture or damage: Unintended damage to organs during procedures
  • Infection: Failure to maintain sterile conditions
  • Anesthesia errors: Incorrect dosing or monitoring of anesthesia
  • Post-operative care failures: Inadequate monitoring or care after surgery

Never Events

Some surgical errors are so egregious they're called "never events"—mistakes that should never happen. These include wrong-site surgery, leaving objects in patients, and performing the wrong procedure. These cases are often easier to prove because the negligence is obvious.

3. Medication Errors

Medication mistakes can occur at multiple points in the healthcare process and can be made by doctors, nurses, pharmacists, or other healthcare providers.

Types of Medication Errors:

  • Prescription errors: Wrong medication, dosage, or duration prescribed
  • Pharmacy errors: Dispensing wrong medication or incorrect dosage
  • Administration errors: Nurses giving medication incorrectly or to wrong patient
  • Drug interactions: Failing to check for dangerous drug combinations
  • Allergy oversights: Prescribing drugs despite known allergies
  • Wrong route of administration: Giving medication orally when it should be IV, etc.
  • Timing errors: Giving medication at wrong intervals

4. Birth Injuries and Obstetric Malpractice

Birth injuries can result from negligence during pregnancy, labor, or delivery. These cases are particularly tragic as they affect both mother and child.

Common Birth-Related Malpractice:

  • Failure to diagnose maternal conditions: Preeclampsia, gestational diabetes, infections
  • Failure to monitor fetal distress: Not recognizing signs of oxygen deprivation
  • Delayed C-section: Waiting too long to perform emergency cesarean
  • Improper use of delivery tools: Forceps or vacuum extractors causing injury
  • Failure to diagnose birth defects: Missing conditions detectable through prenatal testing
  • Medication errors: Wrong drugs or dosages during pregnancy or delivery

Common Birth Injuries:

  • Cerebral palsy from oxygen deprivation
  • Erb's palsy (nerve damage to shoulder and arm)
  • Brain damage from lack of oxygen
  • Bone fractures during delivery
  • Maternal injuries or death

5. Anesthesia Errors

Anesthesia mistakes can be particularly dangerous and even fatal. Anesthesiologists must carefully monitor patients throughout procedures.

Common Anesthesia Errors:

  • Giving too much or too little anesthesia
  • Failing to review patient's medical history for risks
  • Using defective equipment
  • Failing to monitor vital signs during surgery
  • Delayed response to complications
  • Intubation errors
  • Failing to inform patient of pre-operative instructions
⚠️ Serious Consequences: Anesthesia errors can lead to brain damage, stroke, heart attack, permanent injury, or death. Even brief oxygen deprivation can cause catastrophic harm.

6. Failure to Treat

Even with a correct diagnosis, malpractice can occur if a healthcare provider fails to provide appropriate treatment.

Examples Include:

  • Failing to follow up with patients
  • Prematurely discharging patients from hospital
  • Failing to refer to appropriate specialists
  • Not ordering necessary tests or procedures
  • Abandoning a patient
  • Ignoring patient complaints or symptoms

7. Hospital-Acquired Infections

Hospitals have a duty to maintain sanitary conditions and follow infection control protocols. When they fail to do so, patients can develop serious infections.

Common Hospital-Acquired Infections:

  • MRSA (Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus)
  • C. difficile (Clostridium difficile)
  • Surgical site infections
  • Catheter-associated urinary tract infections
  • Central line-associated bloodstream infections
  • Ventilator-associated pneumonia

8. Radiology Errors

Radiologists interpret X-rays, MRIs, CT scans, and other imaging studies. Errors in reading these images can lead to misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis.

Common Radiology Errors:

  • Failing to detect tumors or cancers on imaging
  • Misreading fractures or injuries
  • Failing to compare current images with previous studies
  • Not communicating critical findings promptly
  • Technical errors in performing imaging studies

9. Emergency Room Errors

Emergency rooms are high-pressure environments where mistakes can be particularly dangerous. Common ER malpractice includes:

  • Failing to properly triage patients based on severity
  • Discharging patients prematurely
  • Misdiagnosing heart attacks, strokes, or other emergencies
  • Medication errors in emergency treatment
  • Failing to order appropriate tests
  • Poor communication between ER staff

10. Nursing Home Abuse and Neglect

Elderly patients in nursing homes are vulnerable to various forms of medical malpractice and neglect.

Common Issues:

  • Bedsores (pressure ulcers) from inadequate care
  • Medication errors or over-sedation
  • Falls due to inadequate supervision
  • Dehydration and malnutrition
  • Failure to prevent infections
  • Neglect of basic needs
  • Physical or emotional abuse

11. Failure to Obtain Informed Consent

Before any medical procedure, healthcare providers must obtain informed consent by explaining:

  • The nature of the procedure
  • The risks and benefits
  • Alternative treatment options
  • The consequences of not having the procedure

Malpractice can occur when providers fail to obtain proper informed consent or fail to adequately explain risks, especially when undisclosed risks then occur.

12. Pathology and Laboratory Errors

Mistakes in laboratory testing can lead to misdiagnosis or improper treatment:

  • Contaminated samples
  • Switched samples (testing wrong patient's specimen)
  • Incorrect interpretation of results
  • Delayed reporting of critical results
  • Lost or damaged specimens

Does Your Case Fit These Categories?

If your situation resembles any of these types of malpractice, you may have a valid claim. The next step is understanding what you need to prove. Learn about the essential elements of a medical malpractice case.

Remember that every case is unique. Even if your situation doesn't exactly match these examples, you may still have a valid claim. Consult with a qualified medical malpractice attorney to evaluate your specific circumstances.
